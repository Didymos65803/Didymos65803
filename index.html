About Me

I am an undergraduate student at National Taiwan University with broad research interests, primarily focused on astronomical data analysis. My work spans from studying black hole accretion history to binary asteroids and atmospheric dynamics on Venus.

As an active member of the EASY team and President of the NTU Astronomy Club, I am passionate about both cutting-edge research and science communication. I founded the Hoshiko Plan to bring astronomy to wider audiences.

Publications & Presentations

My academic contributions to the field of astronomy

Papers

Inferring Obscured Cosmic Black Hole Accretion History from AGN Found by JWST/MIRI CEERS Survey
Chien, W.H., et al. (2024)
The Astrophysical Journal, Vol. XX, Issue Y

This study presents the black hole accretion history (BHAH) of obscured active galactic nuclei (AGNs) identified from the JWST CEERS survey using mid-infrared SED fitting. Results show that BHAD peaks between redshifts 1 and 3, with variations depending on the model used.

PDF arXiv DOI
Identifying Binary Asteroids Using Machine Learning with Simulated Lightcurves and FOSSIL Survey Data
Chien, W.H., et al. (2023)
Icarus, Vol. XX, Issue Y

This study presents a machine learning approach using Random Forest classifiers to identify binary asteroids from lightcurves. We aim to study the population of binary asteroids in the main asteroid belt using data from the FOSSIL survey.
